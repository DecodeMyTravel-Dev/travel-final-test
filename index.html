To give you a best estimate breakeven point, this tool utilizes the following 2026 benchmarks:

Baggage Considerations: 2026 Manual

1. The Standard for the "Big Three" (American, Delta, United)

Standard has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17x10x9in. Must fit entirely under seat. Tip: Basic Economy passengers restricted to this.
  • Carry-On: 22x14x9in. Must include wheels and handles in measurement.
  • Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs limit. Fees average $45–$50.
  • Checked Bag (Premium): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags free.

2. Regional Airlines

International regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). Many allow only 10kg for Economy carry-ons.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive ($100–$200 surcharge). Fuel Disclaimers: Variable Fuel Surcharges (YQ/YR codes) and $10-$25 handling fees common.

Currency Intelligence & Port Strategy

1. The Currency Reality Buffer

Standard bank/ATM spreads cost you 3-7%. Our matrix builds in a 4% Reality Buffer so you know the actual cash you will receive after common fees.

2. Airport & Port Traps

Exchange kiosks at airport arrivals are predatory. Expect to lose **12-15%** of value. Machines inside terminals or at cruise piers (e.g., Euronet) charge massive usage fees ($5-$12) and 10% worse conversion rates.

3. Tipping Rules (2026)

Auto-Gratuity: Covers room/dining staff, but NOT shore guides ($10-$20). Port Intelligence: Look for 'Servicio Incluido' or 'Propina' on bills in Mexico/Caribbean before adding extra.
