Baggage Considerations

1. The Standard for the "Big Three" (American, Delta, United)

In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

  • Personal Item: 17x10x9in. Must fit entirely under the seat. Tip: Basic Economy passengers restricted to this item.
  • Carry-On: 22x14x9in. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.
  • Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees average $45–$50.
  • Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags for free.

2. Regional Airlines
Weight Sensitivity: International regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). The Regional Rule: Many allow only 10kg for Economy. Anything over is charged per kilo.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"
Fuel Surcharge (YQ/YR codes) and $10-$25 "Security & Fuel Handling" fees common on regional checked bags at the counter.

Exchange Traps

1. Arrivals Trap: Exchange kiosks (Travelex) are predatory convenience traps. Expect to lose **12-15%** of value.

2. Private ATMs: Machines at cruise piers (e.g., Euronet) charge massive usage fees ($5-$12) and 10% worse conversion rates.

3. Strategy: Walk two blocks away from the port or airport to find a "Mainline" local bank machine for the best rates.
