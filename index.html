Baggage Considerations

1. The Standard for the "Big Three", and many other national and International Airlines

  • American Airlines
  • Delta Airlines
  • United Airlines

In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

Personal Item: 17 x 10 x 9 inches. Must fit entirely under the seat.

Tip: Basic Economy passengers are often restricted exclusively to this item.

Carry-On: 22 x 14 x 9 inches. Must include wheels and handles in your measurement.

Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg) limit. Fees now average $45–$50 for the first bag if not prepaid. A small discount may be applied when purchasing before flight check-in.

Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg) limit. Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags at this weight for free.

2. Regional Airlines

Weight Sensitivity: Unlike US domestic flights, international regional carriers often cap carry-ons at 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). If you are over, they will move it to the hold at the jet bridge.

The Regional Rule: Many Regional carriers (as of 2026) allow only 10kg for Economy and 12kg for Business for carry-ons. Anything over is charged per kilo at the counter.

3. Surcharges & The 2026 "Fuel Factor"

Overweight Fees: Crossing the 50 lb. mark in Economy is expensive ($100–$200 surcharge). Fuel Disclaimers: Variable Fuel Surcharges (YQ/YR codes) and $10-$25 handling fees common.

These guidelines are provided as a best estimate at the time of publishing. Consult your carrier’s website a few days before flying for current limits and pricing.

Global Tipping & Gratuity Manual (2026)

The "Automatic" Trap

In 2026, Auto-Gratuities ($18-$20/day) cover your room steward and dining staff. They do NOT cover shore excursion guides. If you don't tip cash on the pier, the guide receives nothing from your cruise line payment.

Arrivals & ATM Traps

Exchange kiosks at airport arrivals lose 12-15% of value. Machines at cruise piers (e.g., Euronet) charge massive fees ($5-$12) and 10% worse conversion rates. Walk two blocks away from the port or airport to find a "Mainline" local bank machine.

Shore Excursion Etiquette

Drivers: $2-$5 per person. Specialist Guides: $10-$20. Private Drivers: 10-15% of total tour cost.

The Currency Strategy

USD $1 bills are essential for small tips in the Caribbean, Bahamas, and Mexico. In Europe, use local Euro coins—don't tip USD as staff cannot easily exchange small bills.
