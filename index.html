Baggage Considerations

1. The Standard for the "Big Three", and many other national and International Airlines

  • American Airlines
  • Delta Airlines
  • United Airlines

In 2026, the "Standard" has moved to Volumetric Compliance. If your bag doesn't slide into the automated metal sizer without a shove, it's a gate-check.

Personal Item: 17x10x9in. Must fit entirely under seat. Tip: Basic Economy passengers restricted to this.

Carry-On: 22x14x9in. Must include wheels and handles.

Checked Bag (Economy): 50 lbs (23 kg). Fees average $45-$50.

Checked Bag (First/Business): 70 lbs (32 kg). Most premium tickets include 2-3 bags free.

2. Regional Airlines: Caps of 15–22 lbs (7–10 kg). 3. Fuel Factor: Variable Fuel Surcharges (YQ/YR codes) and $10-$25 fees common.

Global Tipping & Gratuity Manual (2026)

The "Automatic" Trap: 2026 Auto-Gratuities cover room/dining staff, but NOT shore excursion guides. If you don't tip cash on the pier, the guide receives nothing.

Shore Excursion Etiquette: Drivers: $2-$5 per person. Specialist Guides: $10-$20. Private Drivers: 10-15%.

Arrival Traps: Airport kiosks lose 12-15% of value. Machines at piers (Euronet) charge $5-$12 fees.
